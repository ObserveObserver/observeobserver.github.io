Thoughts on Dvorak (and welcome!)

Last updated: 7/9/2020.

Welcome to my keyboard blog, I really like keyboards and layouts. Here, I will write a bit about layouts, experiences, new keyboards what-have-you. I hope you like it!

Using programmer dvorak for 2 years, I've realized it's simply not for me; and that's okay. Too many people dismiss the idea of simply abandoning a layout if it doesn't fit them or "flow" right. I will write more sometime about this "flow" as it's something I promised to do a long time ago.

I started dvorak about 2 years ago and quickly switched to programmer, mostly because of the number system. I came to really, really love the symbols as well and think it is a huge benefit (especially when using a lisp.) I liked the layout a lot, but something never felt quite right. Let me get into what I liked about dvorak:

The Good:

1. Nice rolls. Dvorak is an alteration layout, but has some of the best short rolls out there (i.e. th ch gh ns nt gh)

2. Portability. It's everywhere, even on my old Alphasmart I used in school!

3. It's a good layout overall. Really, it is. It has some problems I think, some bad ones too; but it's a fine layout.

4. Punctuation on qwerty QWE. One of my favorite things about the layout!

The Bad:

1. L and R are on terrible fingers. This is really inexcusible imo, and there's nothing to defend it.

2. Overuse of right-hand. Right hand is overused in keypresses and distance dramatically. My righthand sucks (bad genetic rng) so it sucks for me.

3. It just doesn't feel right to me. This is a combination of lots of things, but it just doesn't feel right to me...

Short blog to start it off, but just my reasons why I am not a fan of Dvorak. If it works for you, I'm happy; it just didn't for me.

I will stick with it for the time being, and see if it becomes more comfortable!
